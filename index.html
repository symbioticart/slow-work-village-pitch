The problem

There is no category serving people who need 30+ days of quiet to finish hard work.

Cities are loud

Constant urgency, meetings, notifications, social pressure. Deep-work hours collapse to a few protected fragments.

Hotels are shallow

Built for short stays, entertainment and turnover. No food rhythm, no quiet, no real workspace, no community.

Retreats are programmed

Workshops, cohorts, schedules, networking. Performance, not work. Optimised for short transformation, not finished output.

Founders, writers, researchers and composers describe the same need: distance, silence, time, and a quality environment to finish a serious piece of work. No category serves this directly.

Three converging shifts

  • Senior remote work is normal. Founders and L7+ ICs work from anywhere; "month-on, month-off" is acceptable.
  • Cities are losing the deep-work race. Even the best home office is full of family, errands and noise.
  • Senior peers increasingly judge each other on shipped work, not on travel. Output is the new luxury signal.

Atlantic-Spain advantage

  • Ocean-side site in Galicia or Cantabria — 80 ha of cliff and pine, oceanic climate (mild temperatures year-round; wet, stormy winters).
  • Cost-to-build 20–30% lower than France/Italy; coastal land 40–60% below comparable Mediterranean sites.
  • 1h flight from Madrid to Santiago or Santander, then 30–60 min drive — non-stop flights from London, Berlin, Zurich, NYC to Madrid.
  • Visa-friendly for ICP: Schengen rules cover 30–90 day stays for most target nationalities; Spain Digital Nomad Visa (2023) supports longer relocations.

07 / 26
Category

A new category. Not a hotel, not a retreat, not a coliving.

What it is not

  • Not a hotel. No turnover, no concierge, no daily housekeeping ritual, no entertainment.
  • Not a retreat. No workshops, no facilitators, no cohort, no transformation arc.
  • Not a coliving. No shared kitchens, no common rooms, no events calendar, no party.
  • Not primarily an art residency. Most residents pay and are not asked to produce; we run a small invited residency program on top.
  • Not a digital-nomad hub. Not optimised for short stays or networking; designed for long, quiet work.

What it is

  • A residence. Long stays. Private apartments and houses with their own entrances. A real address while you are there.
  • A working farm. Food rhythm and produce drive the daily structure.
  • A library and a studio. Designed for long-form work, not for performance.
  • A small, slow community. Visible, optional, never compulsory.
  • A place that finishes things. Tracked by a public bibliography of work done on site.

We call this category the production residence: infrastructure organised around finished output, not guest experience.

Operating principles

  • Soundproofing first. Multi-unit residences use double-leaf masonry walls (Rw ≥ 60 dB), independent floor slabs, no shared HVAC ducts.
  • Every unit has a private outdoor entrance. No interior corridors. No "hallway hotel" feel.
  • Solar + battery + ground-source heat covers ~85% of energy by Y2.
  • One central dinner 7 nights a week. Optional. No table assignments.
  • No daily housekeeping. Cleaning on request.
  • No televisions in units. One projection room in the library.

Site selection

Atlantic-Spain coast — Galicia (Costa da Morte / Rías Baixas) or Cantabria (Picos de Europa coastal corridor). ~80 ha with cliff or low-coast access. 1h flight Madrid → Santiago or Santander, then 30–60 min drive. Existing finca conversion gives a further 10–15% capex saving over Spanish greenfield.

Artist residency program

A small, invited residency drives every other part of the business.

How it works

  • 4–6 artists per year, 4–8 weeks each.
  • Stay is free. Travel and per-diem covered for first 3 years; thereafter only stay is free.
  • Curated by an external selection group (one curator + two advisors), refreshed yearly.
  • No teaching obligation. No public performance. They come to work.
  • One open evening per stay — optional, low-key, residents only.
  • One artwork or recording stays with the village.

What it does for the business

  • Press and credibility. Each well-chosen name unlocks 2–4 long-form features per year. Worth €200–400k in equivalent paid acquisition.
  • Waitlist demand. Founders book to be near the artists they admire.
  • Cultural moat. Imitators with money cannot replicate a 5-year body of work made on site.
  • A growing collection. Year by year the village owns a small but real cultural archive.

Cost model: ~€200k/year combined budget — covers ~5 invited artists (~€30k each, full board + per-diem) plus 4 Fellowship places (~€12k each, board only). Funded directly from operating margin. Zero dependency on grants.
